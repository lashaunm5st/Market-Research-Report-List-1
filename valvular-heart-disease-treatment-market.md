<p><p>Valvular heart disease treatment involves various diagnostic and monitoring techniques to assess heart function. Cardiac catheterization offers detailed imaging of heart valves, while electrocardiograms (ECGs) monitor electrical activity, detecting abnormalities. Chest X-rays provide visual insight into heart size and shape alterations. Stress tests evaluate heart performance under physical exertion, helping to identify issues related to valve function. Other methods may include echocardiograms and advanced imaging techniques, each contributing to a comprehensive approach for diagnosis and treatment planning in valvular heart disease.</p></p>
