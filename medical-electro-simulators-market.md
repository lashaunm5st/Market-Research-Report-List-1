<p><ul><li>Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS)</li><li>Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation (EMS)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical electro-simulators market encompasses various types, primarily focusing on Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS) and Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulation (EMS). TENS is used for pain relief by delivering electrical impulses through the skin to stimulate nerves, reducing discomfort. In contrast, EMS targets muscle groups directly, inducing contractions to enhance strength, rehabilitation, or muscle recovery. Both modalities are widely employed in clinical settings and at home, catering to diverse patient needs and improving overall treatment outcomes in pain management and physical therapy.</p></p>
