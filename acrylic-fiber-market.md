<p><p>The acrylic fiber market is primarily divided into two segments: staple and filament. Staple fiber refers to short lengths of acrylic fibers used primarily in textiles, providing softness and warmth in products like sweaters and blankets. Filament fiber consists of continuous strands that are often used in applications requiring durability and strength, such as outdoor fabrics and home furnishings. Both types cater to diverse end-use markets including apparel, home textiles, and industrial applications, driving demand for acrylic fibers.</p></p>
