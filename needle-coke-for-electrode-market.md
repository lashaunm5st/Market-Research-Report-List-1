<p><p>Needle coke is a premium-grade carbon material used primarily in manufacturing electrodes for electric arc furnaces and lithium-ion batteries. The market can be divided into two main types: petroleum-based and coal-based. Petroleum-based needle coke is derived from petroleum distillation and offers superior properties such as lower expansion and higher electrical conductivity, making it suitable for high-performance applications. In contrast, coal-based needle coke is produced from coal tar pitch, generally characterized by lower quality and performance, yet it is often more cost-effective.</p></p>
