<p><p>The craniofacial implants market comprises two primary types: metallic and nonmetallic implants. Metallic craniofacial implants are typically made from biocompatible materials such as titanium or stainless steel, offering durability and structural support for craniofacial repair. In contrast, nonmetallic craniofacial implants are often crafted from materials like polymers or ceramics, providing advantages in terms of aesthetic integration and lower weight. Both types are utilized in reconstructive surgeries, trauma recovery, and aesthetic enhancements, catering to diverse patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
