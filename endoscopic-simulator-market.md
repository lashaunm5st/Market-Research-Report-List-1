<p><p>The endoscopic simulator market is divided into two primary types: flexible and rigid endoscopic simulators. Flexible endoscopic simulators are designed to mimic the characteristics and movements of flexible endoscopes, primarily used in procedures like gastrointestinal and respiratory examinations. In contrast, rigid endoscopic simulators replicate the use of rigid instruments, commonly employed in orthopedic and surgical settings. Both types aim to enhance training for medical professionals, providing realistic practice environments to improve skills, reduce errors, and ultimately enhance patient safety.</p></p>
