<p><p>Smart composites are advanced materials designed to respond to external stimuli, enhancing functionality. Piezoceramics generate electric charge under mechanical stress, ideal for sensors and actuators. Shape memory alloys revert to original shapes upon heating, useful in medical devices and robotics. Magneto-restrictive materials change shape under magnetic fields, enabling precision control systems. Electro-restrictive materials deform under electric fields, suitable for adaptive optics. Carbon and optical fibers offer lightweight, high-strength solutions for aerospace and communication. Thermoelectric materials convert heat to electricity, while thermally responsive materials adapt to temperature changes, both valuable in energy harvesting and smart textiles.</p></p>
