<p><p>The dimmable glass market includes several types, primarily categorized into PDLC (Polymer Dispersed Liquid Crystal) glass and electrochromic glass. PDLC glass switches between opaque and transparent states when an electrical current is applied, offering privacy and glare reduction. Electrochromic glass, on the other hand, changes its tint when voltage is applied, allowing for adjustable light control and energy efficiency. Other market types may include photochromic and thermochromic glass, which respond to environmental light and temperature changes, respectively.</p></p>
