<p><p>The atomized brass powder market features two primary production methods: water atomization and gas atomization. Water atomization involves dispersing molten brass into fine droplets using high-pressure water jets, creating uniform powder particles. This method is cost-effective and suitable for larger production volumes. In contrast, gas atomization utilizes high-velocity gas streams to atomize molten brass, producing finer and more controlled powder particles. This technique is ideal for applications requiring high purity and specific particle sizes, catering to various industries, including electronics and aerospace.</p></p>
